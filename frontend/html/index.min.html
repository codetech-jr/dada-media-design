THE ZAIQA is a Spanish brand that sells cocoa. One of the main challenges I had with this brand was that the client wanted the visual identity not to be pigeonholed into a specific type of product, since in the future it will be a multi-product brand.

THE ZAIQA is a Spanish brand that sells cocoa. One of the main challenges I had with this brand was that the client wanted the visual identity not to be pigeonholed into a specific type of product, since in the future it will be a multi-product brand.

The solution was to create a very conceptual brand focused on the name that means “Flavors.” I designed a system of symbols and shapes that represent the different flavors we can find in the world. Round and square flavors, with depth and without. A pattern made up of different geometric figures was the perfect solution to achieve an adaptable, versatile brand with a good presence.

MAFE repostería is a Colombian personal brand that was created to be adaptable to the professional needs of its owner. The main challenge with this brand was to create a visual identity that would differentiate itself from brands that use kitchen utensils as their only visual resource, using those same resources since the client wanted them in her visual identity.

Through the intense blue color, simplified utensils and focusing on the personal brand and not so much on the product, a visual identity very different from that of the competition was achieved. Even though the brand still has a pink touch, the blue color stands out and gives strength to all visual communication. Many times the error is not in the resources that are used, but in how they are used.
